The Problem

Data is held hostage

Anthem 2015. 78 million records exposed from one breach.

Change Healthcare 2024. A third of Americans' records disrupted.

Your data gets licensed and sold by institutions. You see none of the money.

The gap AI makes urgent

Medical copilots, diagnostic assistants, and autonomous clinical agents all train on health data.

That data is scraped, licensed by institutions, or assembled through opaque partnerships. The patient is never in the loop and never compensated.

No system exists today where a patient owns their records, shares them in a verified and private way, and gets paid — without trusting a centralized entity.

Who Does What

  Medic

Poseidon-hash record into 3 compartments (header / body / PII)

EdDSA-sign the combined commitment over BabyJubJub

Hand commitment + sig to patient (off-chain)

  Patient

List commitment + medic sig on Asset Hub, set price

ECDH encrypt plaintext for buyer's pubkey (Poseidon stream cipher)

Upload ciphertext to Statement Store (People Chain)

Call fulfill() — escrow releases, ciphertext hash on-chain

  Researcher

Place buy order — lock PAS in escrow, register ECDH pubkey

Fetch ciphertext from Statement Store after fulfill

Decrypt with private key + ephemeral pubkey via ECDH

Verify: Poseidon hash ✓   medic EdDSA sig ✓

Why Polkadot

Asset Hub + pallet-revive

Solidity we already know, deployed on a chain with native token settlement and shared security.

Statement Store

Native ephemeral storage for the encrypted ciphertext. No IPFS daemon, no pinning service, no external infra to run.

People Chain

A real on-chain identity layer for physician credentialing via KnownGood judgements. We don't build that ourselves.

Bulletin Chain + DotNS

Frontend hosted on-chain, reachable via own-your-medical-records42.dot.li. No AWS, no Vercel, no DNS registrar.

These aren't ports from another ecosystem. They're primitives this stack has together, and no other stack does.

Honest Flaws

Relaxed atomicity — because verification is off-chain, the patient could send garbage ciphertext and keep the money. A buyer dispute window is next. Not shipped yet.

No on-chain physician identity — medic registry is a multisig-owned contract today. People Chain KnownGood integration is on the roadmap.

Statement Store asymmetry — statement_submit is only available on the People Chain collator on Paseo, not Asset Hub. The hook auto-resolves it — but it's a testnet gap worth filing.

Synthetic data only — no real patients, no legal review. MVP test records only.

What's Next

Close the griefing gap

Buyer dispute window — if the decrypted content doesn't match the commitment, the buyer reclaims their payment.

On-chain physician identity

Wire People Chain KnownGood judgements so buyers can trust the medic key without an off-chain check.

Pilot with real data

Partner with a research group to run a pilot on real, consented records. Legal review required first.

Open to AI buyers

Medical-AI teams as first-class buyers — same privacy and compensation flow, programmatic access.

Sustainability Model

Protocol Fees

1–2% fee on every successful swap, deducted from escrow at fulfill(). Protocol sustains itself from day one — no subsidies needed.

Grant Funding

MVP phase funded by ecosystem grants. Web3 Foundation and Decentralized Futures are the primary targets — both prioritise privacy-preserving infra on Polkadot.

Governance Token (future)

DAO control over fee rate, medic registry governance, and protocol upgrades. Out of scope for MVP — introduced once the protocol has proven traction.

Protocol fees accumulate in a multisig treasury during MVP. Fee rate is hardcoded until DAO governance ships in a future phase.
